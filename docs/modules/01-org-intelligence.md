# Module 01 — Organizational Intelligence Hub

**Project:** OrchestraGrant Platform  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Last Updated:** 2026-05-01

---

## 1. Purpose

The Org Intelligence Hub is the foundation of everything the AI writing engine produces. It is the platform's knowledge base about the organization — a rich, structured profile that replaces a traditional grant consultant's intake questionnaire and years of organizational knowledge. Every narrative generated by the AI is grounded in data from this module.

If an org's profile is incomplete, the AI output will be generic. A complete profile enables specific, compelling, accurate grant narratives.

---

## 2. Onboarding Setup Wizard

New organizations complete a guided setup wizard before accessing the platform. The wizard has 7 sections:

### Section 1: Organizational Identity
- Legal name
- "Also known as" / DBA
- EIN (validated format)
- Year founded
- Legal structure (501c3, fiscal sponsorship, other)
- Brief organizational description (2–3 sentences, used in AI prompts)
- Website, primary phone, primary address

### Section 2: Mission & Vision
- Mission statement
- Vision statement
- Core values (bulleted list, up to 8)
- Primary geographic service area (free text + state/city selector)
- Population(s) served (multi-select + free text for custom)
  - Options: general public, K-12 students, underserved communities, seniors, rural communities, professional musicians, emerging artists, composers, [+add custom]

### Section 3: Ensemble Profile
- Ensemble type (chamber orchestra, full orchestra, sinfonietta, symphony, philharmonic, other)
- Full roster size (number of musicians per service)
- Musician classification (per-service, salaried, hybrid)
- Music director/conductor name and bio paragraph
- Executive Director name, bio paragraph
- Primary venue(s)
- Typical season structure (e.g., "6 subscription concerts + 3 education concerts + 2 summer concerts")

### Section 4: Financial Profile
- Current fiscal year budget (total)
- Previous fiscal year budget (for comparison)
- Earned revenue % vs. contributed revenue %
- Largest revenue sources (ranked top 3)
- Has endowment: yes/no; if yes, endowment value
- Has operating reserves: yes/no; if yes, months of reserves
- Primary financial contact

### Section 5: Programming
- Recent 3-season programs (upload PDFs or enter titles manually)
- Programming emphasis tags (multi-select):
  - American music, world premieres, contemporary works, baroque/historical, standard repertoire, education programming, community engagement, free concerts, pay-what-you-can, touring, recording/broadcast, commissioning
- Average tickets sold per concert
- Annual audience size

### Section 6: Community Impact & Equity
- Community engagement activities (free text, up to 5 bullets)
- DEI statement (free text)
- Demographics of audiences served (if tracked)
- Partnerships with schools, community organizations, healthcare facilities (list)
- Youth programs (names and brief descriptions)

### Section 7: Goals & Strategic Direction
- Current strategic plan period (years)
- Up to 5 strategic goals (short phrases + 1–2 sentence descriptions)
- Upcoming major initiatives (up to 3)

**Wizard completion gating:** Users can skip sections but receive a profile completeness score and a reminder banner until the profile reaches 70%.

## 3. Profile Completeness Scoring

Profile completeness is surfaced throughout the app. Score affects AI output quality.

| Score | Badge | AI Generation Effect |
|---|---|---|
| 90–100% | Complete | Full context provided; specific, compelling output |
| 70–89% | Nearly Complete | Good output; may lack specificity in some areas |
| 50–69% | Needs Attention | Generic output in incomplete areas; warning shown pre-generation |
| < 50% | Incomplete | Warning blocks generation until at least 50% is reached |

### Score Calculation

Points awarded for each completed field group (not individual fields). Major groups:
- Mission & vision: 15 points
- Ensemble profile: 15 points
- Financial profile: 20 points
- Programming: 20 points
- Community impact: 15 points
- Strategic goals: 10 points
- Documents uploaded (at least 1 year of audits, 1 recent annual report): 5 points

## 4. Organizational Profile Detail Page

Beyond the wizard, the profile is maintained via a structured settings area with these sections:

### 4.1 About

Displays the identity, mission/vision, and ensemble profile fields. Editable inline. Version history preserved (see Section 7 below).

### 4.2 Financials

Structured input for each fiscal year:
- Total operating budget
- Total earned revenue (breakdown: ticket sales, education fees, service income, other)
- Total contributed revenue (breakdown: individual, corporate, foundation, government)
- Total endowment value (if applicable)
- Operating reserve balance
- Audit status (audited / reviewed / compiled) and most recent audit date

Budget trend chart (sparkline for last 5 years) displayed on the profile page.

### 4.3 Board

Board roster with for each member:
- Name, title on board
- Board role (chair, vice-chair, treasurer, secretary, at-large)
- Affiliation (employer, title)
- Join date, term expiry
- Committee membership(s)
- Is a donor: yes/no

Board composition summary auto-calculated: total members, % with arts backgrounds, % with legal/financial backgrounds, % demographic diversity (if org chooses to track).

### 4.4 Staff

Staff roster with:
- Name, title
- Department
- Contact (email)
- Start date

### 4.5 Concert Seasons

Season records with:
- Season name/year
- Concert series (list)
- Highlights (world premieres, commissioned works, notable guest artists)
- Season brochure PDF upload

Used by AI engine for programming history context.

### 4.6 Programs & Activities

Persistent list of recurring programs (not single concerts):
- Education programs (Symphony in Schools, Young Artists Competition, etc.)
- Community engagement programs
- Recording/media projects
- Special initiatives

Each has: name, description, year started, primary audiences, annual participants.

## 5. Document Vault

The Document Vault is the document repository connected to the Org Intelligence Hub. Uploaded documents are parsed and used to populate narrative atoms for AI generation.

### 5.1 Supported Document Types

| Document Category | File Types | Processing |
|---|---|---|
| Audited Financial Statements | PDF | LlamaParse → financial data extraction |
| IRS Form 990 | PDF | Structured data extraction (revenue, expenses, compensation) |
| Annual Reports | PDF | Full text + narrative atom extraction |
| Strategic Plan | PDF, DOCX | Full text + strategic atom extraction |
| Grant Applications (past) | PDF, DOCX | Section-level extraction, narrative atoms |
| Board Meeting Minutes | PDF, DOCX | Governance narrative extraction |
| Program Notes | PDF, DOCX | Programming narrative extraction |
| Press Releases | PDF, DOCX, TXT | Public-facing narrative extraction |
| Evaluation Reports | PDF | Impact data extraction |
| IRS Determination Letter | PDF | Stored reference only |
| Bylaws | PDF, DOCX | Stored reference only |
| W-9 | PDF | Stored reference only |

### 5.2 Document Lifecycle

```
Upload (presigned S3 URL)
    │
    ▼
Virus scan (ClamAV)
    │
    ▼
File type validation
    │
    ▼
LlamaParse / pdfplumber (extract text)
    │
    ▼
Chunking + section classification
    │
    ▼
Embedding generation (OpenAI text-embedding-3-large)
    │
    ▼
Narrative atoms stored in ai.narrative_atoms table
    │
    ▼
Document marked as indexed; status = active
```

### 5.3 Document Vault UI

- Grid view of all documents organized by category
- Per-document: status badge (Processing, Indexed, Failed), upload date, uploaded by
- Re-process button: reparse and re-embed the document (e.g., after model upgrade)
- Delete: soft delete; atoms from this document marked inactive; confirm dialog warns about impact on AI generation
- Download: presigned S3 URL for original file

## 6. Narrative Atom Library

Narrative atoms are the extracted chunks of organizational narrative that fuel AI generation. Staff can view, edit, and annotate atoms directly.

### 6.1 Atom View

Browseable list with:
- Search by content (semantic search against atom embeddings)
- Filter by: category, document source, fiscal year, quality score
- Each atom shows: text, source document, section type, embedding status, times used in generation

### 6.2 Atom Editing

Staff can:
- Edit atom text (corrects errors from PDF parsing; re-embeds automatically)
- Add manual atoms (write key facts directly, not sourced from a document)
- Soft-delete atoms that are outdated or inaccurate
- Tag atoms with custom labels

Manual atoms are treated identically to document-extracted atoms in RAG retrieval.

### 6.3 Atom Quality Scoring

Each atom receives a quality score (0–1) based on:
- Length (too short or too long penalized)
- Specificity (presence of numbers, names, dates — scored higher)
- Recency (newer documents scored higher)

Quality score influences retrieval ranking alongside vector similarity.

---

## 7. Profile Version History

All changes to the org profile are logged with timestamp and user. The profile page includes a "View history" button per section. Users can view a diff of any two versions and restore a previous version.

Version history is retained indefinitely.
